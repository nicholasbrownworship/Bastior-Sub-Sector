The Bastior Sub-Sector

Located in the trailing reaches of Segmentum Ultima, the Bastior Sub-Sector comprises seven inhabited star systems bound by ancient pacts of mutual defence and trade. For millennia it has been a prosperous backwater — its forge world producing reliable materiel, its agri-worlds feeding Crusade fleets bound for the Eastern Fringe.

That prosperity ended when the first hive ships were detected crossing the Mandeville point of the Calyx System. Within months, what Imperial intelligence dismissed as a minor tendril had revealed itself as a full feeding tongue of Hive Fleet Leviathan, guided with terrible cunning from below rather than above.

"We have fought the sons of Chaos, the eternal undead, and the arrogant Aeldari — and prevailed. But this is different. This enemy does not want our worlds. It wants to unmake them." — Canoness Superior Yvelline Rast, Order of the Crimson Shroud

The Seven Systems

Forge World
Bastior Prime

The administrative capital of the sub-sector. Its manufactoria hammer out weapons day and night. Currently besieged but holding under the iron resolve of its defenders.

FALLEN — Consumed
Calyx Secundus

Once a thriving agri-world of four billion souls. Now a stripped husk, its topsoil digested and atmosphere fouled with spores. The first confirmed planetary consumption.

Contested
Morthix IV

A teeming hive world of eleven billion. Its underhives had been seeded with Genestealer cults for generations — the sleepers awoke when the Tyranid vanguard arrived.

FALLEN
Ostria Reach

A civilised world, the first to fall to direct assault. The last transmission from Governor Serevant was received 47 days into the campaign. Kill-teams confirmed consumption.

Besieged
Vethanus Station

A vital void station and naval yard. If it falls, the Imperial Navy loses its primary repair and resupply point in the sub-sector. Under constant bio-ship pressure.

Staging Ground
Pentarch

A cold mining world repurposed as a major Imperial staging ground and refugee processing centre. Its deep bunkers shelter millions of displaced persons.

Infiltrated
Rho-Kethis

A shrine and cemetery world, sacred to the Ecclesiarchy. Thought safe due to its lack of biomass — now confirmed to host Lictor and Void-Stalker incursion teams.

✦

Hive Fleet Leviathan — Splinter Tendril Omicron

Unlike the main body of Hive Fleet Leviathan that attacked from below the galactic plane, Splinter Tendril Omicron approaches from the galactic south-east — an angle that Imperial strategists failed to adequately guard. The tendril is estimated at forty-seven capital hive ships and hundreds of smaller bio-vessels.

What makes Omicron particularly dangerous is its apparent tactical intelligence. Rather than headlong consumption, this tendril probes, feints, and isolates — seeming to learn from each engagement. Magos Biologis Evander Kael has theorised that a Norn-Queen of exceptional cognitive development may be directing the tendril.

Imperial Forces in Theatre

  • Iron Wardens Chapter — Third Company deployed in full. Siege specialists holding the outer defence lines of Bastior Prime.
  • Bastior 1st–9th Infantry Regiments — Raised from the forge world itself. They know this industrial terrain better than any outsider.
  • Order of the Crimson Shroud — Two Orders Militant deployed to Rho-Kethis and Morthix IV to combat Genestealer cult activity.
  • Skitarii Legio Bastior — The forge world's augmented warrior servants. Over thirty maniples in active deployment.
  • Battlegroup Severance — A reduced fleet centred on battlecruiser Inexorable Fury, protecting Vethanus Station.
